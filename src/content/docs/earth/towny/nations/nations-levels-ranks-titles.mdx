## Nation Levels

Each nation can progress into multiple stages and unlock benefits.

| Nation Level | Minimum Residents | Nation Upkeep Modifier | Town Upkeep Modifier | Bonus Town Blocks | Bonus Outposts |
|--------------|-------------------|------------------------|----------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Union | 15 | 1 | 1 | 25 | 0 |
| Republic | 30 | 2 | 1.2 | 50 | 1 |
| Protectorate | 45 | 3 | 1.4 | 75 | 1 |
| Kingdom | 60 | 4 | 1.6 | 100 | 1 |
| Dominion | 75 | 5 | 1.8 | 125 | 1 |
| Divine Realm | 90 | 6 | 2 | 150 | 1 |

## Nation Ranks

Within a nation, you can be granted various ranks that come with specific permissions. Being granted a rank is a high privilege and you should try your best to fill the role adequately. Below is a list of ranks and the permissions they come with. Ranks can be given to residents with the command `/t rank add [Username] [Rank]`.

1. **King**:
    * The supreme ruler of the nation.
    * Has full control over all nation actions and decisions.

2. **Assistant**: Can manage the nation, including:
    * Adding or removing allies.
    * Managing enemies.

3. **Treasurer**: Can manage the nation's finances, including:
    * Depositing and withdrawing money from the nation bank.
    * Accessing the nation's financial history.

4. **Helper**: Can add towns to the nation.

## Nation Titles

You can assign customised nation titles to members of your nation. These have no gameplay impact and provide some simple aesthetic and informational flavour.

You can assign these titles via `/n set title [Username] [Title]`. These titles can be given special colours, either via regular Minecraft colour codes (`&[code]`) or a specific hex colour (`<#[hex]>`). Nation titles will override town titles.
